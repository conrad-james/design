ABOUT US

We are a small London based architectural practice working in two distinct fields: Community based design, often for the charitable sector; and private residential design.


We aim to design beautiful, practical, inspiring living environments. Conrad has a wealth of experience in residential design, and has worked on many projects from small loft conversions to large multi unit developments. However the experience of working in economically challenging environments has brought an added rigour and interest in cost effective design.


The community/ development work is often based in developing countries, within a different culture and climate, sometimes with considerable cost constraints and logistical construction challenges. We are interested in low energy passive design, in the use of local materials, in economic construction, in technical innovation, and take inspiration from local vernacular, both traditional and modern. Beyond the technical challenges, human issues necessarily dominate the brief. Here our role is to listen, to take a step back and question assumptions, and to translate thoughts, feelings and human need into form.


Conrad studied architecture at Cambridge University and the Bartlett School of Architecture. He has worked at world class practices, including Morphosis in LA and Foster and Partners in London. He interrupted his career to study the "architecture of rapid change and scarce resources", and has since had a sideline combining an interest in humanitarian architecture in developing countries with creative design. Since 2008 he has consulted for Houston Morris Architects as project architect for many beautiful residential projects.

SERVICES

Blitz design service

You will probably have ideas about what you want, and we have experience, as well as ideas to add. This service is a joint brainstorm to come up with design options. The cost of this service will be deducted from the full service if you decide to proceed.

Feasibility

This is an extension to the blitz design service, testing the outcome in more detail, and may involve conversations with building control officers, planning departments, builders and consultants if required.

Full service

Broadly speaking, the full service can be broken down into several work stages: design development, planning permission, producing a set of tender documents, tender negotiations, contract administration and handover. Some clients only want up to a certain stage, such as obtaining planning permission, or a set of construction drawings, others opt for the full service.
